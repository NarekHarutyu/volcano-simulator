How a Volcano Forms

This simulation shows how a volcano can form through mantle melting and repeated eruptions. Heat inside Earth melts mantle rock, creating magma. Because magma is hot and less dense than surrounding rock, it rises into a magma chamber beneath the crust. As pressure builds, magma travels through a conduit and erupts at the surface. Each eruption deposits lava that cools into a new layer, gradually building the volcano cone over time.

Step Through the Process

  1. Mantle melting

    Heat and pressure deep inside Earth melt mantle rock into magma.

  2. Magma rises

    Hot, less-dense magma rises buoyantly through the crust.

  3. Pressure builds

    Magma collects in a chamber. Pressure climbs until it forces upward.

  4. Eruption

    Magma travels up the conduit and erupts as lava and ash.

  5. Lava layers cool

    Each flow cools into a new layer, slowly building the cone.
